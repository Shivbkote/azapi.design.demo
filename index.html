FAQs

What is an OCR service?
An OCR (Optical Character Recognition) service is a tool that converts different types of documents, such as scanned paper documents, PDFs, or images captured by a digital camera, into editable and searchable data.
How does CAPTCHA verify human users?
CAPTCHA (Completely Automated Public Turing test to tell Computers and Humans Apart) works by presenting a challenge, like recognizing distorted text or identifying objects in images, that humans can solve easily but automated bots struggle with, ensuring only real users access a service.
Can OCR be integrated with CAPTCHA?
Yes, OCR services can be integrated with CAPTCHA systems to enhance the accuracy of recognizing and processing text in images, especially for automated processes that need to verify text-based CAPTCHAs.
What are the use cases for OCR + CAPTCHA?
Common use cases include automating data entry, enhancing document management systems, ensuring secure user authentication, and improving accessibility by converting images into readable text.
Is OCR data processed securely?
Yes, most OCR services implement robust security measures to ensure that the data processed is encrypted and protected from unauthorized access, safeguarding sensitive information.
